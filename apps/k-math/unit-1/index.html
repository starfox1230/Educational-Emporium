Lesson 1.1: The One and Only! ‚ú®

(Book Reference: Page 14)

Lesson Objectives

  • Recognize a quantity of 1.
  • Trace and identify the numeral 1.

Materials Needed

  • Physical: 1 small toy (like a race car or plastic animal), Paper for tracing (optional, if not using digital).
  • App Provides: Interactive activities for quantity 1, digital tracing for numeral 1.

Activity: Introduce 1

Start by saying:

"Today, we're going to learn all about the amazing number 1!"

Place 1 small toy on the table (or show a picture of one if in the app).

"Look! This toy is all by itself. There's just 1 toy."

Move the toy (or image).

"No matter where I put it, there's still just 1 toy."

Continue with these prompts:

"Can you hold up 1 finger?" (Child holds up 1 finger)

"Look at my face. I have 1 nose. Can you point to your 1 nose?" (Child points)

"I have 1 mouth. Can you point to your 1 mouth?" (Child points)

"Can you clap your hands 1 time?" (Child claps once)

"Can you balance on 1 foot?" (Child tries to balance)

Activity: Find Groups of 1

"You have just 1 nose and just 1 mouth. Let's look around the house (or in this picture) for things that we have only 1 of."

Encourage the child to find single objects. Examples: one refrigerator, one favorite blankie, one cookie left.

Workbook (Page 1): Write 1

"We can write numbers, just like we write letters and words! If I'm making a grocery list and need just 1 apple, this is how I write the number 1."

Show how to trace/write the number 1 (app will demonstrate this digitally).

"To write a 1, we start at the top and make a long line straight down. Easy peasy!"

Guide the child to complete the workbook page or the digital tracing activity. Remind them to say "one" as they trace.
